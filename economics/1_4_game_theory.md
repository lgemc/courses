# ECON 1050 - Strategy, Conflict, and Cooperation

## Overview
This course studies game theory and interdependent decision-making with applications across economics, psychology, politics, and law. Students learn to analyze strategic situations where outcomes depend on the actions of multiple decision-makers.

## Core Topics

### Fundamentals of Game Theory
- What is a game?
- Players, strategies, and payoffs
- Normal form and extensive form games
- Information structures
- Simultaneous vs. sequential games

### Solution Concepts
- Dominant strategies
- Nash equilibrium
- Subgame perfect equilibrium
- Mixed strategy equilibrium
- Rationalizability

### Classic Games
- Prisoner's Dilemma
- Battle of the Sexes
- Chicken game
- Stag Hunt
- Coordination games

### Sequential Games
- Game trees
- Backward induction
- Credible threats and promises
- First-mover advantage
- Stackelberg competition

### Repeated Games
- Finitely repeated games
- Infinitely repeated games
- Folk theorems
- Cooperation and punishment
- Trigger strategies

### Incomplete Information
- Bayesian games
- Beliefs and types
- Bayesian Nash equilibrium
- Signaling games
- Screening games

### Bargaining Theory
- Nash bargaining solution
- Rubinstein alternating offers
- Bargaining power
- Outside options
- Applications to negotiations

### Auction Theory
- First-price auctions
- Second-price auctions
- Revenue equivalence
- Winner's curse
- Auction design

### Cooperative Game Theory
- Coalition formation
- Core and Shapley value
- Transferable utility
- Voting games
- Fair division

## Applications

### Economics
- Oligopoly competition (Cournot, Bertrand)
- Market entry and exit
- Price wars
- Product differentiation
- Advertising strategies

### Political Science
- Voting behavior
- Electoral competition
- Coalition governments
- International relations
- Arms races and disarmament

### Law
- Litigation and settlement
- Contract design
- Crime and punishment
- Regulatory compliance

### Psychology and Social Behavior
- Trust and reciprocity
- Altruism and fairness
- Experimental game theory
- Behavioral game theory
- Evolution and learning

### Business Strategy
- Strategic alliances
- Competitive positioning
- Negotiation tactics
- Supply chain coordination

## Learning Objectives
- Analyze strategic interactions systematically
- Apply game theory to real-world situations
- Predict outcomes in strategic settings
- Design mechanisms and institutions
- Understand limits of game-theoretic reasoning

## Prerequisites
ECON 10A or equivalent; basic mathematics
