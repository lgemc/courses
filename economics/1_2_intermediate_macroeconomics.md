# ECON 1010B - Intermediate Macroeconomics

## Overview
This course covers theories and evidence on economic growth and fluctuations. Topics include analysis of GDP, investment, employment, interest rates, and fiscal/monetary policies with more rigorous modeling than introductory macroeconomics.

## Core Topics

### National Income Accounting
- GDP measurement and components
- National income identities
- Real vs. nominal variables
- Price indices and inflation measurement

### Long-Run Economic Growth
- Solow growth model
- Capital accumulation and steady state
- Technological progress
- Human capital and productivity
- Convergence theory

### The Labor Market
- Labor supply and demand
- Wage determination
- Types of unemployment
- Natural rate of unemployment
- Labor market institutions

### Money and Inflation
- Money supply and demand
- Quantity theory of money
- Inflation causes and costs
- Hyperinflation case studies
- Central bank credibility

### Short-Run Fluctuations
- IS-LM model
- Aggregate demand and supply
- Business cycle theories
- Keynesian vs. classical perspectives
- Real business cycle theory

### Consumption and Investment
- Consumption functions
- Permanent income hypothesis
- Life-cycle model
- Investment theory
- Accelerator model

### Monetary Policy
- Central bank operations
- Interest rate targeting
- Taylor rule
- Unconventional monetary policy
- Zero lower bound issues

### Fiscal Policy
- Government spending multipliers
- Taxation and economic activity
- Budget deficits and debt
- Ricardian equivalence
- Automatic stabilizers

### Open Economy Macroeconomics
- Exchange rate determination
- Balance of payments
- Mundell-Fleming model
- International capital flows
- Currency crises

## Mathematical Tools
- Dynamic modeling
- Difference equations
- Phase diagrams
- Comparative statics

## Learning Objectives
- Understand macroeconomic theories and models
- Analyze economic growth determinants
- Evaluate monetary and fiscal policies
- Interpret macroeconomic data and indicators
- Apply models to current economic issues

## Applications
This course helps analyze:
- Central bank policy decisions
- Government budget policies
- Economic recessions and recoveries
- International economic events
- Long-run prosperity determinants

## Prerequisites
ECON 10B; calculus recommended (Mathematics 1A or equivalent)
