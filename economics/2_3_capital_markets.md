# ECON 1723 - Capital Markets

## Overview
This course provides an introduction to finance including time discounting, risk and return, market efficiency, arbitrage, and financial crises. Students learn fundamental concepts for understanding financial markets and investment decisions.

## Core Topics

### Time Value of Money
- Present and future value
- Discount rates and interest rates
- Annuities and perpetuities
- Compounding frequency
- Real vs. nominal rates

### Risk and Return
- Expected return calculation
- Risk measurement (variance, standard deviation)
- Risk aversion and utility
- Risk premium
- Sharpe ratio

### Portfolio Theory
- Diversification benefits
- Portfolio variance and correlation
- Efficient frontier
- Mean-variance optimization
- Modern Portfolio Theory (Markowitz)

### Capital Asset Pricing Model (CAPM)
- Beta and systematic risk
- Security Market Line
- CAPM assumptions and predictions
- Empirical tests of CAPM
- Alternatives to CAPM

### Market Efficiency
- Efficient Market Hypothesis (EMH)
- Weak, semi-strong, and strong form efficiency
- Random walk hypothesis
- Anomalies and challenges to EMH
- Behavioral finance critiques

### Arbitrage
- Law of one price
- Arbitrage opportunities
- Arbitrage Pricing Theory (APT)
- Limits to arbitrage
- Convergence trades

### Fixed Income Securities
- Bond pricing and yields
- Duration and convexity
- Term structure of interest rates
- Yield curve theories
- Credit risk and ratings

### Equity Valuation
- Dividend discount models
- Price-to-earnings ratios
- Free cash flow valuation
- Comparable company analysis
- Growth vs. value stocks

### Derivatives
- Forwards and futures
- Options (calls and puts)
- Put-call parity
- Black-Scholes model introduction
- Hedging strategies

### Financial Crises
- Asset bubbles and crashes
- Banking crises
- Systemic risk
- Contagion effects
- Crisis case studies

### Historical Crises

#### The Great Depression (1929)
- Stock market crash
- Bank failures
- Policy responses

#### 2008 Financial Crisis
- Subprime mortgages
- Securitization and CDOs
- Lehman Brothers collapse
- Government interventions
- Regulatory reforms

#### Other Crises
- Dot-com bubble (2000)
- Asian Financial Crisis (1997)
- Long-Term Capital Management (1998)
- European Debt Crisis (2010s)

### Market Microstructure
- Bid-ask spreads
- Market makers and liquidity
- Trading mechanisms
- High-frequency trading
- Price discovery

### Behavioral Finance
- Cognitive biases
- Overconfidence and herding
- Prospect theory
- Momentum and reversal
- Sentiment indicators

### International Finance
- Exchange rate determination
- Currency risk
- International diversification
- Capital flows
- Emerging markets

## Empirical Methods
- Event studies
- Time series analysis of returns
- Cross-sectional regression
- Panel data in finance
- Interpreting financial data

## Learning Objectives
- Understand fundamental finance concepts
- Analyze investment opportunities
- Evaluate risk and return tradeoffs
- Apply valuation models
- Interpret market movements and crises

## Applications
This course prepares students for:
- Investment analysis
- Portfolio management
- Risk management
- Financial regulation
- Further study in finance

## Prerequisites
ECON 10A; calculus and statistics recommended
