# ECON 1535 - The Global Economy

## Overview
This course examines the recent wave of protectionism and international trade as viewed through different economic frameworks. It explores trade theory, trade policy, and globalization's effects on the world economy.

## Core Topics

### Classical Trade Theory
- Absolute advantage (Adam Smith)
- Comparative advantage (David Ricardo)
- Gains from trade
- Production possibility frontiers
- Terms of trade

### Neoclassical Trade Models
- Heckscher-Ohlin model
- Factor price equalization
- Stolper-Samuelson theorem
- Rybczynski theorem
- Leontief paradox

### New Trade Theory
- Economies of scale and trade
- Imperfect competition
- Product differentiation
- Intra-industry trade
- Network effects

### Firm Heterogeneity
- Melitz model
- Firm-level trade patterns
- Exporting vs. domestic firms
- Global value chains
- Offshoring and outsourcing

### Trade Policy Instruments
- Tariffs and quotas
- Subsidies and export promotion
- Non-tariff barriers
- Trade remedies (anti-dumping)
- Voluntary export restraints

### Political Economy of Trade
- Median voter and trade policy
- Special interest politics
- Lobbying for protection
- Trade adjustment assistance
- Winners and losers from trade

### Regional Integration
- Free trade areas
- Customs unions
- Common markets
- Trade creation vs. trade diversion
- Regional trade agreements

### Major Trade Agreements
- World Trade Organization (WTO)
- North American Free Trade Agreement (NAFTA/USMCA)
- European Union
- Trans-Pacific Partnership
- Bilateral trade agreements

### Protectionism
- Historical waves of protectionism
- Smoot-Hawley Tariff

- Recent US-China trade war
- Brexit and trade

### Globalization Effects

- Labor market impacts
- Manufacturing decline
- Service sector globalization

### Trade and Development
- Export-led growth

- Agricultural trade and development
- Technology transfer

- Real vs. nominal exchange rates

- Currency manipulation
- J-curve effect

### Balance of Payments

- Trade deficits and surpluses

### Multinational Corporations
- Foreign direct investment (FDI)

- Horizontal vs. vertical FDI

### Trade and Environment

### Trade in Services
- Services trade liberalization

## Learning Objectives
- Understand trade theory fundamentals
- Analyze trade policy effects
- Evaluate globalization impacts

- Critically assess protectionist arguments

This course examines:
- Trade war consequences
- Regional integration benefits
- Globalization and inequality
- Trade policy design
- Future of international commerce
