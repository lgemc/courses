# ECON 70 - Personal Finance: Making Better Decisions and Building a Better Financial System

## Overview
This practical course addresses financial management challenges facing individuals and society. Topics include student loans, credit cards, mortgages, retirement savings, and the broader financial system.

## Core Topics

### Financial Decision-Making
- Time value of money
- Present and future value calculations
- Risk and return tradeoffs
- Behavioral biases in financial choices

### Debt Management
- Student loan strategies
- Credit card usage and pitfalls
- Debt consolidation options
- Credit scores and reports

### Housing Finance
- Renting vs. buying decisions
- Mortgage types and terms
- Down payments and PMI
- Refinancing strategies

### Retirement Planning
- Compound interest and long-term saving
- 401(k) and IRA accounts
- Social Security benefits
- Pension systems

### Investment Basics
- Stocks, bonds, and mutual funds
- Diversification principles
- Asset allocation strategies
- Index funds vs. active management

### Insurance
- Health insurance fundamentals
- Life insurance types
- Disability and property insurance
- Risk management principles

### The Financial System
- Banks and financial institutions
- Regulation and consumer protection
- Financial crises and systemic risk
- Reforms for a better financial system

## Learning Objectives
- Make informed personal financial decisions
- Understand key financial products and services
- Avoid common financial mistakes
- Evaluate financial system policies and reforms

## Applications
This course helps students:
- Build a sound personal financial plan
- Navigate major financial decisions
- Understand how the financial system affects individuals
- Advocate for financial system improvements

## Prerequisites
None; open to all students
