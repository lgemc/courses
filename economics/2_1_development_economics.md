# ECON 1343 - The Economics of Development and Global Health

## Overview
This course harnesses development and health economics literature to approach fundamental questions about poverty and health inequality. It examines both theoretical frameworks and empirical evidence from developing countries.

## Core Topics

### Development Economics Fundamentals
- Measuring poverty and inequality
- Human Development Index
- Poverty traps
- Economic growth in developing countries
- Institutional quality and development

### Health Economics
- Health production functions
- Demand for health and healthcare
- Healthcare financing in poor countries
- Disease burden measurement (DALYs)
- Cost-effectiveness analysis

### Education and Human Capital
- Returns to education
- Education quality and access
- Child labor and schooling decisions
- Gender gaps in education
- Education policy interventions

### Labor Markets in Developing Countries
- Informal sector employment
- Migration and remittances
- Labor market discrimination
- Unemployment and underemployment
- Agricultural labor markets

### Credit Markets and Financial Inclusion
- Microfinance and microcredit
- Credit constraints
- Savings behavior
- Mobile banking and fintech
- Insurance markets

### Agriculture and Rural Development
- Agricultural productivity
- Land tenure and reform
- Technology adoption
- Market access for farmers
- Food security

### Health Interventions
- Disease prevention programs
- Vaccination campaigns
- Maternal and child health
- Malaria, HIV/AIDS, tuberculosis
- Nutrition interventions

### Governance and Institutions
- Corruption and development
- Political economy of reform
- Decentralization
- Property rights
- Rule of law

### Randomized Controlled Trials
- Experimental methods in development
- RCT design and implementation
- External validity concerns
- Ethics of experimentation
- Famous development RCTs

### Policy Applications
- Cash transfer programs
- Conditional cash transfers
- Deworming programs
- Bed net distribution
- Teacher incentives

## Global Health Challenges

### Infectious Diseases
- Epidemiology basics
- Disease transmission models
- Public health interventions
- Vaccine economics
- Antibiotic resistance

### Non-Communicable Diseases
- Rising burden in developing countries
- Risk factors and prevention
- Healthcare system capacity
- Chronic disease management

### Health Systems
- Primary healthcare delivery
- Health workforce challenges
- Pharmaceutical supply chains
- Universal health coverage
- Public-private partnerships

## Learning Objectives
- Understand causes of poverty and inequality
- Analyze health challenges in developing countries
- Evaluate development interventions
- Apply economic theory to global health issues
- Critically assess empirical evidence

## Applications
This course examines:
- Foreign aid effectiveness
- Global health programs (WHO, Gates Foundation)
- Poverty alleviation strategies
- Health policy in low-income countries
- Sustainable Development Goals

## Prerequisites
ECON 10A, ECON 10B recommended
