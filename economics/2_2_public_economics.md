# ECON 1410 - Public Economics: Designing Government Policy

## Overview
This course analyzes the government's role in a market economy, focusing on US tax and budgetary issues. It examines both theoretical justifications for government intervention and practical policy design.

## Core Topics

### Market Failures and Government Intervention
- Externalities and Pigouvian taxes
- Public goods provision
- Natural monopolies
- Information asymmetries
- Merit goods and paternalism

### Public Goods Theory
- Non-rivalry and non-excludability
- Free-rider problem
- Optimal provision of public goods
- Lindahl equilibrium
- Mechanism design for public goods

### Externalities
- Positive and negative externalities
- Coase theorem
- Pigouvian taxes and subsidies
- Cap-and-trade systems
- Environmental regulation

### Taxation Theory
- Equity vs. efficiency tradeoffs
- Tax incidence analysis
- Deadweight loss of taxation
- Optimal tax theory
- Ramsey rule

### Tax Systems
- Income taxation
- Payroll taxes
- Corporate taxation
- Consumption taxes (VAT, sales tax)
- Property and wealth taxes
- Estate and gift taxes

### Tax Policy Design
- Tax base and rate structure
- Progressive vs. flat taxes
- Tax credits and deductions
- Earned Income Tax Credit
- Child Tax Credit

### Social Insurance Programs
- Social Security system
- Unemployment insurance
- Medicare and Medicaid
- Disability insurance
- Program design and sustainability

### Healthcare Policy
- Market failures in healthcare
- Adverse selection and moral hazard
- Health insurance mandates
- Affordable Care Act
- Single-payer vs. private insurance

### Social Security
- Pay-as-you-go vs. funded systems
- Intergenerational transfers
- Demographic challenges
- Retirement age and benefits
- Privatization proposals

### Government Spending
- Cost-benefit analysis
- Infrastructure investment
- Education spending
- Defense expenditures
- Transfer programs

### Budget Policy
- Deficit financing
- Government debt sustainability
- Fiscal rules and constraints
- Balanced budget amendments
- Crowding out effects

### Income Redistribution
- Welfare economics and equity
- Income inequality measurement
- Cash vs. in-kind transfers
- Means-testing
- Work incentives

### Behavioral Public Economics
- Nudges and choice architecture
- Default effects in policy
- Commitment devices
- Tax salience
- Retirement savings behavior

### Political Economy
- Median voter theorem
- Special interest politics
- Lobbying and campaign finance
- Bureaucracy and public choice
- Voting paradoxes

## Policy Applications

### Current US Tax Debates
- Tax reform proposals
- Corporate tax rates
- Capital gains taxation
- Wealth tax debates
- International tax competition

### Entitlement Reform
- Social Security solvency
- Medicare cost control
- Long-term care financing
- Pension systems

### Fiscal Sustainability
- Long-term budget projections
- Debt-to-GDP ratios
- Generational accounting
- Tax gap analysis

## Learning Objectives
- Understand economic rationales for government policy
- Analyze tax and spending programs
- Evaluate policy tradeoffs
- Apply economic theory to policy design
- Interpret empirical evidence on policy effects

## Prerequisites
ECON 1010A recommended; ECON 10A minimum
