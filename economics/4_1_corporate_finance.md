# ECON 1745 - Corporate Finance

## Overview
This course introduces corporate finance including capital budgeting, capital structure, dividend policy, and takeovers. It examines how firms make financial decisions and how these decisions affect firm value.

## Core Topics

### Firm Valuation
- Discounted cash flow (DCF) analysis
- Free cash flow calculation
- Weighted average cost of capital (WACC)
- Terminal value estimation
- Valuation multiples (P/E, EV/EBITDA)

### Capital Budgeting
- Net present value (NPV)
- Internal rate of return (IRR)
- Payback period
- Profitability index
- Project selection and ranking

### Investment Decision Rules
- NPV rule superiority
- IRR pitfalls
- Incremental cash flows
- Sunk costs and opportunity costs
- Real options

### Capital Structure Theory
- Modigliani-Miller theorems
- Irrelevance propositions
- Tax shield benefits
- Financial distress costs
- Optimal capital structure

### Debt vs. Equity
- Trade-off theory
- Pecking order theory
- Market timing
- Agency costs of debt
- Leverage effects

### Cost of Capital
- Cost of equity (CAPM)
- Cost of debt
- After-tax cost of debt
- WACC calculation
- Adjusting for risk

### Dividend Policy
- Dividend irrelevance (MM)
- Clientele effects
- Signaling theory
- Share repurchases vs. dividends
- Payout policy trends

### Agency Problems
- Shareholder-manager conflicts
- Shareholder-bondholder conflicts
- Free cash flow problem
- Executive compensation
- Corporate governance

### Mergers and Acquisitions
- Merger motives (synergies, etc.)
- Valuation in M&A
- Merger waves
- Takeover defenses
- Leveraged buyouts (LBOs)

### M&A Process
- Due diligence
- Deal structure
- Payment methods (cash vs. stock)
- Merger arbitrage
- Post-merger integration

### Corporate Restructuring
- Divestitures and spin-offs
- Carve-outs
- Leveraged recapitalizations
- Bankruptcy and reorganization
- Distressed investing

### Financial Distress
- Costs of financial distress
- Bankruptcy process (Chapter 7 vs. 11)
- Debt restructuring
- Creditor rights
- Vulture investors

### Working Capital Management
- Cash management
- Inventory management
- Accounts receivable policy
- Short-term financing

### Risk Management
- Hedging strategies
- Currency risk
- Interest rate risk
- Commodity price risk

### International Corporate Finance
- Foreign exchange exposure
- Political risk

- International capital budgeting
- Transfer pricing

### Behavioral Corporate Finance
- Managerial overconfidence
- Market timing
- Investor sentiment effects
- Mergers and hubris
