# ECON 10A - Principles of Economics (Microeconomics)

## Overview
Economists study human behavior using a combination of models and data. This foundational course introduces students to economic models using intuitive discussions, graphical analysis, and basic algebra. The focus is on individual decision-making and markets.

## Core Topics

### Individual Decision-Making
- Consumer behavior and preferences
- Utility maximization
- Budget constraints
- Rational choice theory

### Markets and Pricing
- Supply and demand fundamentals
- Market equilibrium
- Price mechanisms
- Elasticity concepts

### Economic Models
- Model construction and interpretation
- Graphical analysis techniques
- Basic algebraic formulations
- Real-world applications

### Market Structures
- Perfect competition
- Monopoly
- Oligopoly
- Market failures

## Learning Objectives
- Understand how individuals make economic decisions
- Analyze market outcomes using supply and demand
- Apply economic models to real-world situations
- Develop analytical thinking skills using graphs and algebra

## Applications
This course provides essential tools for understanding:
- Consumer choices and market behavior
- Business pricing decisions
- Government policy impacts on markets
- Resource allocation in society

## Prerequisites
Basic algebra and graphical analysis skills
