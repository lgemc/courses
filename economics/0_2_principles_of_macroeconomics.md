# ECON 10B - Principles of Economics (Macroeconomics)

## Overview
This course continues the economics curriculum from ECON 10A, shifting focus to macroeconomics. It examines the overall economy including growth, business cycles, and economic crises.

## Core Topics

### Economic Growth
- Long-run economic growth theories
- Factors driving productivity
- Capital accumulation
- Technological progress

### Business Cycles
- Economic fluctuations
- Recessions and expansions
- Leading and lagging indicators
- Cyclical patterns in the economy

### National Income Accounting
- Gross Domestic Product (GDP)
- Components of GDP
- Real vs. nominal GDP
- Economic indicators

### Unemployment and Inflation
- Types of unemployment
- Natural rate of unemployment
- Inflation measurement (CPI, PPI)
- Phillips Curve relationship

### Monetary and Fiscal Policy
- Central banking and monetary policy
- Interest rates and money supply
- Government spending and taxation
- Policy effectiveness and limitations

### Economic Crises
- Financial crises origins
- Banking system vulnerabilities
- Policy responses to crises
- Historical crisis case studies

## Learning Objectives
- Understand macroeconomic indicators and their interpretation
- Analyze economic growth and fluctuations
- Evaluate monetary and fiscal policy impacts
- Examine causes and consequences of economic crises

## Applications
This course provides frameworks for understanding:
- National economic performance
- Government economic policies
- Central bank decisions
- Global economic trends

## Prerequisites
ECON 10A or equivalent microeconomics foundation
